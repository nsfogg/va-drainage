Downspout

A downspout is a pipe that carries rainwater from a roof's gutter system down to the ground or into a drainage system. It's an essential component of a building's roofing and drainage system, designed to channel the water collected by gutters away from the building's foundation. Downspouts help to prevent soil erosion, water damage to the building's structure, and flooding in basements or around the building's foundation. They can be made of various materials like aluminum, copper, or PVC, and are often connected to a gutter at the roof's edge to efficiently manage rainwater runoff.
